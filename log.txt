The Core Problem
Manual note-taking during meetings and lectures is tedious and often results in missed information. The goal of this challenge is to build a web or mobile application (a "clone" of an existing app called Summary) that completely automates this process using AI.

What You Need to Build
You need to develop an application that takes audio as an input and outputs highly structured, useful text.

1. The Inputs
Your app must allow users to provide audio in two ways:
Live Recording: Capture audio directly through the app during a live meeting or lecture.
File Import: Upload a pre-recorded audio file.

2. The AI Processing & Outputs
Once the audio is received, your app needs to process it and automatically generate the following:
Full Transcript: A word-for-word text record of the audio that includes speaker timestamps (identifying who spoke and when).
TLDR & Summary: A brief "Too Long; Didn't Read" overview, followed by a more detailed bullet-point summary of the discussion.
Key Decisions: A clear list of any final decisions made during the recording.
Action Items: A generated list of tasks or next steps that were discussed.

3. Required App Features
Beyond just processing the audio, the application must include:
Meeting History: A dashboard where users can view and search through all their past recordings and notes.
Export Functionality: An easy way to send the generated notes out of your app and into tools like Notion or Google Docs.

How You Will Be Evaluated
If you are submitting this for the $50 prize, the judges are focusing on three main areas:
Transcription Accuracy: How perfectly the AI translates the spoken audio into text.
Summary Quality: How well the AI understands the context to generate useful summaries and action items.
Export UX (User Experience): How smooth and intuitive it is for the user to export their final notes to other platforms.
